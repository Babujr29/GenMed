The Root Problems

1. Knowledge Gap

61% of patients believe generic medicines have lower quality than branded medicines
75% of patients unaware of Jan Aushadhi scheme
Only 3% of medicines are actually substandard (affects both generic and branded equally)

2. Prescription Practices

Only 5.82% generic prescribing rate in India (vs 84.6% in USA, 86.1% in Brazil)
16.66% of doctors actually prescribe generic medicines despite 100% awareness
Doctors influenced by pharmaceutical company branding and relationships

3. Accessibility Issues

Limited availability of generic medicines
Only 230 Jan Aushadhi stores expanded slowly
Retailer profit margins exceeding 68% on branded medicines
Information asymmetry between healthcare providers and patients

Research Foundation

Our solution is grounded in extensive academic research and evidence-based approach to ensure credibility and effectiveness.

Evidence-Based Approach

Quality Assurance (Singal et al., 2011; Aivalli et al., 2018): Generic and branded medicines from same manufacturers show identical quality
Cost Savings Potential (Sovani et al., 2025; George & Baliga, 2021): 80-85% cost reduction validated across multiple studies
Behavioral Insights (Tripathi & Bhattacharya, 2018; Dongre et al., 2010): Knowledge gap is primary barrier, not quality concerns
Policy Support (Mehra et al., 2024; Dixit et al., 2018): Government actively promoting generic medicines with regulatory framework

Research-Backed Evidence

Key Findings from Research:

• 97% of medicines meet statutory quality standards (both generic and branded)

• Laboratory testing confirms identical bioavailability

• Patients respond positively when properly informed about generics

• Government policies actively support generic substitution
